Technique Categories

Kicks (Chagi)

Front Kick (Ap Chagi): A forward-directed kick using the ball of the foot.
Side Kick (Yop Chagi): A lateral kick delivered with the edge of the foot.
Roundhouse Kick (Dollyeo Chagi): A circular kick targeting the body or head.
Back Kick (Dwit Chagi): A kick delivered backward targeting the opponent behind.
Hook Kick (Huryeo Chagi): A kick with a circular motion, striking with the heel.

Punches (Jireugi)

Jab Punch (Jab Jireugi): A quick, straight punch with the leading hand.
Cross Punch (Cross Jireugi): A straight punch with the rear hand.
Hook Punch (Hook Jireugi): A punch with a curved motion, targeting the opponent's side.
Uppercut Punch (An Chireugi): A punch directed upward, underneath the opponent's guard.

Blocks (Makgi)

Low Block (Arae Makgi): A blocking technique used to defend against lower attacks.
High Block (Ulgul Makgi): A block to protect against high-level attacks.
Inner Block (An Palmok Makgi): A block using the inner forearm.
Outer Block (Bakat Palmok Makgi): A block using the outer forearm.

Forms (Poomsae or Patterns)

Taegeuk Poomsae: A series of choreographed movements representing various aspects of Taekwondo philosophy and techniques.
Black Belt Poomsae: Advanced forms performed by black belt practitioners.

Self-Defense (Hosinsul)

Joint Locks: Techniques that involve manipulating joints to control or immobilize an opponent.
Throws: Techniques that involve off-balancing an opponent and taking them to the ground.
Pressure Points: Application of force to specific points on the body for control or submission.

Sparring (Gyeorugi)

Free Sparring: Controlled, dynamic exchanges between practitioners.
One-Step Sparring: Practicing predetermined techniques in response to specific attacks.

Strikes (Jeorugi)

Knife-Hand Strike (Sonkal Taerigi): A striking technique using the edge of the hand.
Back Fist Strike (Dung Joomuk Taerigi): A strike delivered with the back of the fist.
Palm Heel Strike (Sonbadak Taerigi): A strike using the palm of the hand.

Stances (Seogi)

Front Stance (Ap Seogi): A forward-facing stance with a longer stance length.
Back Stance (Dwit Seogi): A backward-facing stance with a shorter stance length.
Horse-riding Stance (Juchum Seogi): A low, stable stance resembling riding a horse.
